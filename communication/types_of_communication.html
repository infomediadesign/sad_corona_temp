Types of Communication

Basic Forms of Communication

We can distinguish between the following basic forms of communication:

  • Persistent vs. transient communication
  • Asynchronous communication - the sender continues its computation immediately after the transmission of its message
  • Synchronous communication - the sender is blocked until its message is stored in a local buffer at the recipient host or delivered to the recipient.

E.g. Client/server communication is usually based on synchronous communication. This has disadvantages:

  • The client cannot perform any other work while waiting for a response
  • Failures must be dealt with immediately
  • in many cases this model just not appropriate as client and server have to be active at the time of communication (e.g. e-mail or messengers)
