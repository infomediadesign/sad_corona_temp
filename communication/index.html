Communication in Distributed Systems and Applications

Protocols are the basis of every distributed system or application. However, there are some disadvantages when it comes to the design and development of complex distributed applications.

Firstly, network protocols are based on message-oriented communication and do not allow other communication models. Furthermore, a higher degree of abstraction is advantageous when designing distributed applications. Accordingly, network protocols offer many details per se, but at the lowest level. Thus they also violate the concept of transparency.

This is where middleware provides common services and protocols for the development of distributed applications, e.g.:

  • high‐level communication services ( e.g. Remote Procedure Call)
  • security protocols (e.g for authentication and authorization)
  • distributed locking protocols for mutual exclusion
  • distributed commit protocols
